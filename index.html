Prof. Haruzo Hida had told me that, when he was at the Institute of Advanced Study, among the luminaries like Weil, Shimura, Borel, Langlands it was Harish Chandra who he felt a little intimidating for he always stood upright. He puts him on the same pedastal as Ramanujan and Brahmagupta. Born in Kanpur, Harish Chandra's influential papers in Lie Groups and Representation theory occupies a central position in Modern Mathematics. To commemorate his legacy, I came up with the name Seminaire Harish Chandra modelled upon the French Seminaire Nicolas Bourbaki.
